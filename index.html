About the event

A Maker Space culture, one that emphasizes creation over memorization, open source sharing over knowledge, and process over conduct, gives new ways for students to explore concepts and show what they know. MAKE-A-THON 1.0 is a 48 hour Hackathon scheduled in Youthopia’19 the Techno-Cultural fest of DIT University. The participants would be asked to develop something out of scratch or innovate any idea, Software or Hardware based and complete the development task in the given period of time. Each teams would be allotted some virtual money and they’ll have to purchase the components with that virtual money. Using those components each team will have to develop something innovative and out of the box. The team coming out with the best idea and with the best utilization of resources will take a lead.

Rules

  • Team-member

    Each Team would have 3 members.

  • Creativity

    A project can be anything innovative and constructive it may be hardware or software based or a combination of both.

  • Virtual Money

    A Team would be allotted some virtual money with which they can purchase resources for their project like Electronic Components (like Arduino, Rasp Pi, and Sensors etc.), Software, APIs and Mentorship.

  • Uniquenes

    The idea should not be copied.

  • Time

    The project should be made in the given time frame at the venue only and should started from scratch, any prior work in the project may lead to disqualification of the team.

  • No extra money

    No extra virtual money would be provided to any team, and teams will have to plan their resources accordingly.

  • List of resources

    The complete list of resources will be disclosed only on the day of event and participants will frame their project accordingly.

  • Laptops

    Participants must bring their own laptops if needed.

  • Softwares and API's

    All software, APIs and hardware like electronic components will be provided here only.

  • Powerpoint presentation

    Teams will have to prepare a 5-6 slide PowerPoint presentation for the presentation on final day

  • Time of presentation

    Teams would be given a time of 2 minutes on the final day to present their project to the jury and 30 seconds for answers any question by the jury. No extra time would be given to any team and extending their presentation may lead to deduction of points.

  • Fine

    Any damage to any property of the college or any component would be eligible for fine and charges.

FAQ's

What is a MAKEATHON?

A hackathon can be best described as an “invention marathon”, or in simple words hackathons are about bringing your crazy ideas to reality. It’s an event where any tech-enthusiast, regardless of their individual field can come & participate to learn, build & share their creations over the course of a weekend, in a relaxed & productive environment. Hackathons are simply a venue for self-expression & creativity, where people come together & transform their thoughts into reality, through technology.

How big a team can be?

The maximum team size is three and lone rangers are also welcome.

Who all can participate?

This is a student hackathon and only students are allowed to participate. You’ll be required to verify at the event by showing your student ID card.

I am a newbie, can I hack?

Of course you can! We ensure that no one is left behind during the hacking, thanks to our mentors and volunteers who help us achieve the same. We make sure that you learn the best you can alongside having fun and experiencing what it feels to belong to a community.

Can I start working on my hack before the event?

No. In the interest of fairness, students should not be working on their projects before Makeathon begins and we do not allow participants to work on pre-existing projects. However, you can familiarize yourself with all the tools and technologies you intend to use beforehand.

Do I need to have any specific qualifications to be a participant for the hackathon?

If you love to hack, you are more than welcome to participate in the hackathon.

What to bring to the hackathon?

Please bring a valid university ID (or any student ID if in high school), a computer (preferably a laptop), chargers, and leave hardware on us! No firearms, weapons, alcohol, or illegal drugs are allowed on campus.

Does Make-A-Thon provides Internet facility during hackathon?

Yes, you would be provided internet connection during hackathon either via LAN or WiFi
